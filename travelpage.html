Getting Around Bagan

Best Ways to Get Around

Bagan offers countless ways to explore...

Traditional Cow Cart

Horse Carriage Ride

Hot Air Balloon Ride

Bicycles and motorbikes are the most common way of travelling in Bagan. They allow you travel at your own speed, are inexpensive, and are simple to ride. They are ideal for short distances & are frequently available for rental.

You may want to hire a private vehicle or taxi for the day for a more comfortable experience, particularly in the summer. If you're visiting temples that are widely apart, taxi and car are fantastic choice.

A horse cart and cow cart trip is a traditional experience. It is slower than other options, but it gives you more time to unwind and take in the view.

For a truly magical experience, take to the skies at sunrise and sunset in a hot-air balloon. Enjoy breathtaking views of the temple-studded plains. Rides operate from November to March and must be booked in advance.

When to Travel & Best Time of Day

Enhance your visit with the perfect timing and experiences

Sunrise at Bagan

Hot Air Balloon over Bagan

The best time to visit Bagan is at the time of dry season (November-February). The water is cool with the clear blue skies. It is perfect for cycling, temple visits, hot-ari balloon rides. Book accomodations and activites early because it is the busiest time of the year.

Hot season is during (March–May). It has very high daytime temperatures. Early morning and late afternoon are the most comfortable times to explore during hot season.

Monsoon season is during (June–October). Rain showers are common, but the landscape turns lush and green. There are fewer tourists. Balloon rides are less frequent because of the wet weather conditions.

The most enchanting time of day is in the early morning (5:30–8:30 AM). Beautiful sunrises, less crowds, and milder temps at dawn. Hot-air balloon rides also depart.  It is the time to check up on sunrise in Bagan.

At sunset (5:30–6:30 PM), the the temples' golden illumination from the setting sun. For this iconic scene, a large number of tourists congregate at designated views or temple terraces.
