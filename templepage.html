“The temples in Bagan were primarily constructed using fired bricks and mud-based mortars with vegetal binders; some structures of temples are also incorporating sandstone for reinforcement.” Golden temples are built using a combination of materials, with the use of gold leaf, diamonds, and rubies to cover brick and copper structures. There are 2 main architectural styles used in building temples in Bagan. They both took their influence from early Pya people’s constructions in India and Sri Lanka (Ceylon), and they represent Mount Meru in the Buddhist cosmos. Stupa-style (Solid temples) are bell-shaped with one or more rectangular terraces at the base. They contain a relic chamber.
