Bangan was the first capital of the Burmese empire. King Anawrahta implemented the Bagan dynasty. He is the first King to introduce the Buddhist religion in Myanmar.

The Bagan Dynasty is also known as the Pagan Dynasty. The Burmese culture, language, ethnicity, territory and architecture started to be shaped in Bagan and the architecture led to the evolution of the identity of the community.

Bagan is located in an area where there are many strong earthquakes ( 7- 8.5 Richter scale). The Bagan dynasty was founded under King Anawrahta in the middle of the 11th century. The kingdom is located in Old Bagan. Old Bagan is located on the banks of the Ayeyarwady River.

Today, Bagan is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and remains one of Myanmar’s most treasured cultural landmarks, attracting visitors from around the world who come to explore its timeless beauty.

Known for its historic city of more than 3,500 Buddhist pagodas, temples, and monasteries, Bagan, Myanmar, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. An early, significant Buddhist empire's level of religious commitment is reflected in the remarkable collection of Buddhist monumental architecture found in Bagan. Bagan stands out and is unique in the rich traditions and expressions of Buddhist architecture and art that can be seen all throughout Asia.

Bagan has a remarkable collection of Buddhist monumental architecture which  holds the depth of a significant early Buddhist empire's religious commitment. Bagan is unique among the many Buddhist architectural and artistic expressions and traditions that can be found throughout Asia.
